Working with me, your mentor

As soon as your IPP process begins, contact me to schedule your first meeting!

My job is to guide you and offer feedback on your progress. I oversee several student portfolios, so it is your responsibility to schedule meetings before your deadlines.

Don't procrastinate! If there is a problem with your portfolio when you present it for your final checkpoint, this may delay your graduation.

Checkpoint #1: Establish your objective

Before meeting, think about who your portfolio is for - potential employers. What impression do you want to make on them? What positions will you be applying for?

Bring with you:

  • Your written objective
  • Initial planning: sketches, wireframe, concepts
  • If you have an existing portfolio, bring it, too!

Checkpoint #2: Curate your work

After your first meeting, you left the table with a set of tasks to accomplish. Bring your completed tasks and any additional work you have done for your portfolio.

Bring with you:

  • Sample work to be included in your portfolio.
  • More concrete planning: comps, mockups
  • Your code progress

Checkpoint #3: Final Approval

For final approval, your online portfolio must be finished, and contain:

  • A clear objective
  • A well-organized, beautifully presented collection of your best work, with descriptions of each project objectives
  • A complete, up-to-date résumé
  • A working contact page
  • All of the above hosted on your own domain
